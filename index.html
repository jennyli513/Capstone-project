Description

The purpose of mentoring is to tap into the existing knowledge, skills, and experience of senior or high performing students and transfer these skills to newer or less experienced students in order to advance their academic performance.

Types of Mentoring

• Traditional mentoring: One senior student to one junior student.
• Group mentoring: One senior student to up to four junior students.
• Team mentoring: Several senior students working with small groups of junior students in which the senior students to junior students’ ratio is not greater than 1:4.

Outcome for mentor

• A certificate of Appreciation for the Mentor

Mentor recruiting process

• Selecting mentors can be achieved by letting students know about the process.
• There are a few traits that good mentors share:
1. high performers (achieved grade B and above)
2. the ability to facilitate knowledge
3. the ability to transfer skills
4. active listening and communication skills
